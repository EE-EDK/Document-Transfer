## Key Milestones to Expect

| Domain | Milestone | Typical Range |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Visual** | Tracks objects to midline, fixates on faces at 8-12 inches | 0-2 months |
| **Motor (Gross)** | Lifts head briefly during tummy time, turns head side to side | 1-3 months |
| **Motor (Fine)** | Reflexive palmar grasp, hands mostly fisted | 0-2 months |
| **Social** | Social smile, responds to voice, quiets when held | 6-8 weeks |
| **Language** | Cooing, vowel sounds, responds to loud sounds | 2-3 months |

## Sensory and Motor Protocols

### Visual Stimulation
- **High-contrast cards:** Black and white geometric patterns, held 8-12 inches from face
- **Why:** The fovea centralis is immature; photoreceptor cells and the optic nerve require high-contrast stimulation to drive myelination (AOA)
- **Frequency:** 2-3 short sessions daily (3-5 minutes), following the infant's alertness cues
- **Progression:** Begin with simple shapes (circles, stripes). By 6-8 weeks, introduce bullseye patterns and face-like arrangements. By 10-12 weeks, begin adding red as the first color the visual system can reliably process

> See [domains/sensory-motor.md](../domains/sensory-motor.md) for full visual stimulation protocol

### Tummy Time with Mirror
- **Unbreakable acrylic floor mirror** placed during supervised prone positioning
- **Why:** AAP recommends tummy time from shortly after birth to prevent positional plagiocephaly and build cervical/lumbar strength. The mirror provides a dynamic visual target exploiting the infant's preference for face-like structures
- **Frequency:** Start with 1-2 minutes, 2-3 times daily. Build toward 15-20 minutes total daily by end of month 3
- **Technique:** Place mirror at slight angle so infant can see own face. Position high-contrast cards near mirror for additional stimulation

### Tactile and Grasping
- **Varied texture objects** placed in palms during alert periods
- **Why:** NIH outlines the progression from reflexive palmar grasp to intentional grasping. Diverse tactile input facilitates neural pathway development
- **Materials:** Soft fabric swatches (silk, terry, fleece), smooth wooden rings, soft rubber grasping toys
- **Note:** All grasping at this stage is reflexive. The goal is sensory exposure, not intentional manipulation

> See [domains/sensory-motor.md](../domains/sensory-motor.md) for full tactile protocol

## Philosophical Application

### Parental Modeling Phase
At this stage, the philosophical framework applies to the parents, not the infant. The infant is absorbing emotional tone, cadence, and resolution patterns.

**Dichotomy of Control:**
- When frustrations arise (sleep deprivation, feeding difficulties, schedule disruptions), verbalize the framework aloud: "I can't control that she woke up. I can control how I respond right now."
- The infant cannot understand the words but absorbs the emotional regulation pattern — calm tone following frustration, resolution after disruption

**Articulation:**
- Narrate actions and emotions to the infant constantly. "I'm changing your diaper now." "You seem hungry." "That startled you."
- This serves dual purpose: language exposure for the infant and habit formation for the parent

**Local Order:**
- Maintain a predictable, organized environment. Consistent sleep space, feeding area, play area
- Predictable routines (not rigid schedules) for wake, feed, play, sleep cycles

## Language and Reading

### Approach: Neurological Language Acquisition
- **Read aloud daily** — content matters less than exposure to prosody, rhythm, and varied phonemes
- **Recommended materials:** Board books with high-contrast images, rhythmic text, repetition
- **Method:** Slow, exaggerated prosody. Pause at page turns. Let the infant look at images
- **Target:** At least 15-20 minutes of reading daily, split across sessions

### Suggested Books (0-3 months)
- *Black White* by Tana Hoban
- *Look Look!* by Peter Linenthal
- *Hello, World!* series (high contrast editions)
- *Goodnight Moon* by Margaret Wise Brown (for prosody/rhythm)

> See [domains/reading-curriculum.md](../domains/reading-curriculum.md) for full book lists

## Environment and Routine

- **Sleep:** Follow AAP safe sleep guidelines — firm, flat surface, no loose bedding, room-sharing without bed-sharing
- **Feeding:** On demand. Track feeding patterns but do not impose rigid schedules
- **Play space:** Designate a consistent area with a play mat. Rotate sensory objects weekly to maintain novelty without overwhelming
- **Noise:** Background music during alert/play periods (classical, varied instrumental). Silence during sleep transitions

> See [domains/environment-order.md](../domains/environment-order.md) for space design details

## Warning Signs (Consult Pediatrician)

- No social smile by 8 weeks
- Does not track objects or faces by 2 months
- No startle response to loud sounds
- Cannot lift head at all during tummy time by 3 months
- Persistent fisting of hands with no relaxation by 3 months
- Not feeding adequately or consistent weight loss

> See [domains/nutrition-sleep.md](../domains/nutrition-sleep.md) for feeding benchmarks
