## Known Source Conflicts and Resolutions

### Conflict 1: Stoic Apatheia vs. Modern Emotional Processing

**The tension:** Traditional classical Stoicism advocates for suppression or complete detachment from certain emotional states (*apatheia*). Modern developmental psychology (AAP, APA) emphasizes the necessary experiencing and processing of emotions for healthy development.

**Resolution:** The Stoic Dichotomy of Control is applied strictly as a mechanism for cognitive behavioral regulation — specifically, *how one responds to an emotion and the external event that triggered it*. It is **not** applied as a mandate for emotional suppression. The sequence is:

1. The emotion is acknowledged ("You feel frustrated")
2. The emotion is validated ("That makes sense")
3. The cognitive framework is applied ("Can you change what happened? No. What can you choose to do now?")

This aligns with the APA's recommended approach to pediatric emotional development while preserving the practical utility of the Stoic framework.

### Conflict 2: Demanding Articulation vs. Developmental Readiness

**The tension:** The Principle of Truth and Articulation requires precise speech. But children develop expressive language on variable timelines, and excessive pressure on verbalization can create anxiety (ASHA guidelines).

**Resolution:** Articulation demands are calibrated strictly to developmental stage and individual readiness. The principle is aspirational, not punitive. A child who cannot yet articulate is helped, not withheld from. The sequence is:

1. Create space for the attempt
2. Model the correct articulation
3. Accept approximations appropriate to age
4. Gradually raise the bar as capacity grows

### Conflict 3: Structured Environment vs. Creative Exploration

**The tension:** Maintenance of Local Order emphasizes organization and predictability. But developmental psychology (particularly Piagetian theory) emphasizes the importance of unstructured, exploratory play for cognitive development.

**Resolution:** Order applies to the organizational framework (things have places, routines have structure), not to the content of play itself. Within an ordered environment, play is free and exploratory. The child knows where materials are, what the routine is, and what happens next — this predictability *enables* deeper exploratory play because cognitive resources are not consumed by environmental uncertainty.

## How to Use This Document

When any claim in the plan is questioned or revisited:

1. Identify which source supports it (check the relevant stage or domain document)
2. Find that source's authority level in this document
3. Apply appropriate confidence: Level 1-2 claims are treated as reliable; Level 3-4 claims are applied with awareness that they represent theoretical or philosophical positions
4. Check the conflict resolutions section if the claim sits at an intersection of competing frameworks
