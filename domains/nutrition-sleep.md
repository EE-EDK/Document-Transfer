# Nutrition and Sleep

Feeding guidelines, sleep science, and schedule patterns to support optimal physical and cognitive development.

---

## Sleep

### Why Sleep Matters for Development
Sleep is when the brain consolidates learning, forms long-term memories, and undergoes critical neural development. Insufficient or disrupted sleep directly impairs:
- Memory consolidation (learning from the day is literally lost)
- Emotional regulation (sleep-deprived children show increased irritability, tantrums, and reduced frustration tolerance)
- Physical growth (growth hormone is primarily released during deep sleep)
- Immune function

### Sleep Requirements by Age (AAP/AASM Guidelines)

| Age | Total Sleep (24 hrs) | Nighttime | Naps |
|-----|---------------------|-----------|------|
| 0-3 months | 14-17 hours | Variable | Variable (no pattern yet) |
| 3-6 months | 12-16 hours | 10-12 hours | 3-4 naps |
| 6-12 months | 12-16 hours | 10-12 hours | 2 naps |
| 12-18 months | 11-14 hours | 10-12 hours | 1-2 naps |
| 18-36 months | 11-14 hours | 10-12 hours | 1 nap |
| 3-5 years | 10-13 hours | 10-12 hours | 0-1 nap (drops by age 5) |
| 5-7 years | 9-12 hours | 9-12 hours | None |

### Safe Sleep (0-12 Months) — AAP Guidelines

**Non-negotiable:**
- **Back to sleep** — always placed on back for every sleep
- **Firm, flat surface** — no pillows, blankets, bumpers, or soft objects in crib
- **Room sharing without bed sharing** — infant sleeps in own crib/bassinet in parent's room for at least first 6 months (ideally 12)
- **No loose bedding** — use a sleep sack/wearable blanket instead
- **No overheating** — dress in one layer more than adult would wear

### Sleep Environment

| Feature | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Temperature** | 68-72°F (20-22°C) |
| **Light** | Dark for sleep. Dim red/warm nightlight if needed for feeds |
| **Sound** | White noise machine (consistent, not too loud — 50-60 dB, placed away from crib) |
| **Consistency** | Same crib, same room, same pre-sleep routine |

### Sleep Routine Principles
- **Consistent bedtime** — same time (±15 min) every night
- **Wind-down sequence** — 20-30 minutes of progressively calming activities
- **No screens** — at least 1 hour before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin)
- **Last feed before routine** — avoid feeding to sleep as sole sleep association after 6 months
- **The routine IS the sleep cue** — bath → pajamas → book → song → bed becomes the signal

### Common Sleep Challenges

**4-month sleep regression:** Normal. Sleep architecture is reorganizing. Maintain routines, expect disruption for 2-4 weeks.

**8-10 month regression:** Often tied to separation anxiety and motor milestones (crawling, standing). The child practices new skills in the crib. Maintain boundaries gently.

**18-month regression:** Coincides with language explosion and growing autonomy. May resist bedtime. Hold the routine firmly and calmly.

**Night waking (all ages):** Brief wakings are normal and biological. The question is whether the child can return to sleep independently. By 6+ months, most healthy infants can learn to self-soothe with gradual approaches.

## Nutrition

### Feeding Timeline

| Age | Primary Nutrition | Notes |
|-----|------------------|-------|
| 0-6 months | Breast milk or formula exclusively | WHO/AAP recommend exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months if possible |
| ~6 months | Introduction of solids alongside breast milk/formula | Signs of readiness: sits with support, shows interest in food, loss of tongue-thrust reflex |
| 6-9 months | Purees → mashed → soft finger foods | Introduce variety early: vegetables, fruits, grains, proteins |
| 9-12 months | Increasing texture complexity, self-feeding begins | Offer water in open cup, continue breast milk/formula |
| 12+ months | Transition to table foods, whole milk if weaning | Three meals + 2 snacks typical pattern |
| 2+ years | Family meals, varied diet | Child serves self with guidance |

### Allergen Introduction (Current AAP Guidance)
- **Introduce common allergens early** (around 6 months, not later): peanuts, eggs, dairy, wheat, soy, tree nuts, fish, shellfish, sesame
- Early introduction reduces allergy risk (LEAP study, 2015)
- Introduce one new allergen every 2-3 days to identify reactions
- Consult pediatrician if family history of allergies or eczema

### Feeding Philosophy

**Ellyn Satter's Division of Responsibility** (widely endorsed by pediatric nutrition experts):

> **The parent decides:** What food is offered, when, and where
> **The child decides:** Whether to eat and how much

**Why this works:**
- Avoids power struggles over food
- Builds the child's internal hunger/fullness regulation
- Prevents picky eating patterns caused by pressure
- Aligns with the philosophical framework: the child exercises control over what is within their domain (eating), while the parent manages the environment (what's available)

### Practical Feeding Guidelines

**Infant (6-12 months):**
- Offer variety without pressure
- Let the infant explore food (texture, taste, mess is part of learning)
- Model eating — eat together when possible
- No honey before 12 months (botulism risk)
- No added salt or sugar

**Toddler (12-36 months):**
- Expect food preferences to narrow (neophobia — fear of new foods peaks around age 2)
- Continue offering rejected foods without pressure — research shows 10-15 exposures may be needed before acceptance
- Family meals at the table. Same food for everyone (adapted textures as needed)
- Structured meal and snack times (3 meals + 2 snacks). Avoid grazing
- Allow messiness — self-feeding develops fine motor skills and autonomy

**Early Childhood (3-5 years):**
- Involve the child in food preparation (washing vegetables, stirring, pouring)
- Discuss nutrition simply: "This helps your muscles grow. This gives you energy to run."
- No food as reward or punishment
- The child can serve themselves from family dishes (builds self-regulation of portions)

**School Age (5-7 years):**
- Basic cooking skills with supervision
- Understanding of food groups and balanced meals
- Pack their own lunch (with guidance on requirements)
- Continued family meals as priority — conversation and connection, not just nutrition

### Hydration
- **0-6 months:** Breast milk/formula provides all needed hydration. No water needed
- **6-12 months:** Small amounts of water in open cup with meals (2-4 oz/day)
- **12+ months:** Water freely available throughout the day. Milk at meals
- **Limit juice:** AAP recommends no juice before 12 months, max 4 oz/day ages 1-3, max 6 oz/day ages 4-6

## The Meal Environment (Connection to Local Order)

- **Consistent location:** Meals happen at the table, not in front of screens
- **Consistent timing:** Predictable meal and snack schedule
- **Family meals:** Eat together as often as possible. This is a primary social learning environment
- **Conversation:** Meals are a natural conversation opportunity. Practice articulation, share experiences, discuss the day
- **Participation:** Age-appropriate involvement in preparation, setting the table, and cleaning up
- **No screens during meals** — meals are for eating, conversation, and connection
